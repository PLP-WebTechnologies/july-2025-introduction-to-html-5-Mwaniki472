1.Introduction

Floriculture in Kenya is a thriving industry, known for its diverse range of flowers and high-quality production standards. The country is one of the leading exporters of cut flowers in the world, particularly to Europe and the Middle East.

3.History of Floriculture in Kenya

The history of floriculture in Kenya dates back to the 1960s when the government recognized the potential of the flower industry. Over the years, it has grown into a major economic sector, providing employment and contributing to foreign exchange earnings.

4.Types of Flowers Grown in Kenya

Kenya is known for a wide variety of flowers, including:

  • Roses
  • Chrysanthemums
  • Gerberas
  • Alstromeria
  • Orchids
  • Lilies

These flowers are cultivated in various regions across the country, benefiting from Kenya's favorable climate and altitude.

5.Conclusion

Floriculture in Kenya continues to thrive, with ongoing investments in technology and sustainable practices. The industry plays a crucial role in the country's economy and is poised for further growth in the coming years.
